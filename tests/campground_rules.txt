JOSHUA TREE NATIONAL PARK — CAMPGROUND RULES & REGULATIONS

SECTION 1: QUIET HOURS
All campgrounds observe quiet hours from 10:00 PM to 6:00 AM. During quiet hours,
noise must be kept to a level that does not disturb neighboring campsites. Generators,
loud music, and amplified sound are strictly prohibited during quiet hours. Violations
may result in citation or removal from the campground.

SECTION 2: CAMPFIRES & FIRE SAFETY
Campfires are permitted ONLY in designated fire rings or grills provided at each campsite.
Ground fires, including the use of fire pans, are prohibited throughout the park.
Firewood collection within the park is prohibited; campers must bring their own firewood
or purchase it from approved vendors. All fires must be fully extinguished before leaving
the campsite or going to sleep. Water should be poured over the fire, stirred, and
checked for heat. Fire extinguishers are recommended.

SECTION 3: WILDLIFE & FOOD STORAGE
All food, coolers, and scented items must be stored in hard-sided vehicles or approved
bear-resistant containers when not in use. Do not leave food unattended on picnic tables.
Feeding wildlife is strictly prohibited. Violation carries a fine of up to $5,000.

SECTION 4: VEHICLE & PARKING REGULATIONS
Each campsite is limited to two vehicles and six people maximum. Additional vehicles
must use the overflow parking area. All vehicles must remain on paved or designated
roads. Off-road driving is strictly prohibited.

SECTION 5: TRAIL ETIQUETTE
Stay on marked trails at all times. Shortcutting switchbacks accelerates erosion.
Dogs are NOT permitted on any trails within the park. Dogs are allowed in campgrounds
and along paved roads only and must be on a leash no longer than 6 feet at all times.

SECTION 6: DRONE POLICY
The use of unmanned aircraft systems (drones) is prohibited within Joshua Tree National
Park boundaries. This applies to all areas including campgrounds, trails, and backcountry.

SECTION 7: CHECKOUT & CAMPSITE
Checkout time is 12:00 PM (noon). Campsites must be left clean and free of trash.
All personal belongings, trash, and equipment must be removed by checkout time.
The "leave no trace" principle applies — pack out everything you pack in.

SECTION 8: ALCOHOL POLICY
Alcoholic beverages are permitted at campsites only. Public consumption of alcohol
on trails, at trailheads, or in parking areas is prohibited.

SECTION 9: GENERATOR USE
Generators may only be operated between 7:00 AM and 9:00 PM. Generator use is
not permitted during quiet hours (10:00 PM to 6:00 AM).

SECTION 10: GROUP SIZE
Groups of 8 or more require a special group camping permit obtained in advance.
Standard campsites accommodate a maximum of 6 people and 2 vehicles per site.
